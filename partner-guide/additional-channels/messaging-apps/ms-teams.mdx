# Overview

The Microsoft Teams integration enables you to receive critical MoEngage alerts directly within a designated channel in your workspace.

## MoEngage × MS Teams

The integration of MoEngage and Microsoft Teams allows you to configure Teams as an alert destination. By routing key alerts—such as campaign statistics, flow performance, and token expirations—into your team's communication hub, you create a unified, real-time feed for monitoring marketing activities. With this integration, you can do the following:

  * Receive real-time notifications for critical marketing alerts without switching applications.
  * Centralize campaign and system health monitoring in your primary collaboration tool.
  * Enable immediate team collaboration on triggered alerts.



## Use Cases

Integrating Microsoft Teams as an alert destination helps you with the following use cases:

  * **Real-time incident response** : Instantly notify the relevant stakeholders when critical metrics (like delivery rates) drop or when tokens (APNS/Facebook) are about to expire, reducing downtime.
  * **Centralized monitoring** : View campaign performance stats and flow execution details alongside your daily team discussions.
  * **Collaborative troubleshooting** : Discuss and resolve alert triggers immediately within the Teams channel, ensuring transparency and faster resolution.



# Integration

</Info>

## Step 1: Create an MS Teams Channel

  1. Log in to your MS Teams workspace.
  2. Navigate to Chat in the left navigation and click **+**.
  3. On the **Create a channel** dialog box, enter the following details: 
     1. In the **Add the channel to a team** field, select the preferred team name.
     2. In the **channel name** box, enter your channel name.
     3. In the **Description** box, enter your channel description. 
     4. From the **Choose a channel type** list, select _Standard- everyone on the team has access_.
     5. Click **Create**.  
![MSTEAMSS.png](/images/partner_42946330018580.png)



## Step 2: Generate a Webhook URL

  1. On the channels page, click the ellipsis icon on the right and select **Workflows**.  
![WORKFLOW1.png](/images/partner_42946330019092.png)
  2. On the **Workflows** dialog box, select _Send webhook alerts to a channel_.  
![workflows2.png](/images/partner_42946330019732.png)
  3. On the Send webhook alerts to a channel page, ensure the app has a valid connection. The app has a valid connection if a checkbox is visible next to the Microsoft Teams organization account name.
  4. Click **Next**.  
![workflow3.png](/images/partner_42946330020244.png)
  5. In the **Details** section, configure the following fields: 
     1. **Microsoft Teams Name** : Select the team that contains the channel you created.
     2. **Microsoft Teams Channel** : Select the specific channel you created previously. 
  6. Click **Workflow**. When the workflow is added successfully, a confirmation message appears.
  7. In the message, click the Copy icon to copy the workflow URL.  
![workflow4.png](/images/partner_42946334946196.png)  
This Webhook URL enables programmatic message delivery to the chosen MS Teams channel through your Teams app. Copy the generated URL to integrate it into your alert settings.



<Info>
Workspace administrator approval may be required to generate this Webhook URL. You can optionally message the admin to request approval.
</Info>
## Step 3: Integrate MS Teams into Alert Management via Webhook URL

  1. On the left navigation menu of your MoEngage UI, click **App marketplace**.   
![slackkkkkkk.png](/images/partner_42946334952596.png)
  2. On the App marketplace page, click **Alert management** , and then select **Microsoft Teams**.  
![MSTEAMMSS.png](/images/partner_42946334954004.png)
  3. On the **Microsoft Teams** page, click the **Integrate** tab.
  4. On the **Integrate** tab, enter the following details: 
     * **Connection name** : Enter the connection name.
     * **Connection URL** : Enter the Webhook URL created in the [step 2](https://help.moengage.com/hc/en-us/articles/39608405575316#h_01JSXK60169F5PWQNGC91SDKR3).  
![msteams.gif](/images/partner_42946330027156.png)
  5. Click **Connect**.
  6. After the connection is defined, you can now select the defined new destination from the **Send Alerts On** list while creating an alert on the [Alert management ](/user-guide/settings/reports-and-alerts/alert-management/getting-started/create-an-alert)page. You can select _Email_ , _MS Teams,_ or both.  
![ghsxghsagh.png](/images/partner_42946330027924.png)
  7. After adding the destinations, you can view the defined alert destinations on the [Alert management](/user-guide/settings/reports-and-alerts/alert-management/getting-started/create-an-alert) page.  
![hsvcghsHCX.png](/images/partner_42946330028436.png)



<Info>
* Alerts sent to MS Teams are webhook-triggered.
  * When alerts are configured for both email and MS Teams, unsubscribing from email notifications will not affect the delivery of MS Teams alerts, which will continue to be sent.
</Info>
