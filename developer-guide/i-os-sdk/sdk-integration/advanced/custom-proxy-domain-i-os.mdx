title: 'Custom Proxy Domain - iOS'
---

<p>
  In today's privacy-focused digital landscape, many users employ ad blockers or
  private DNS services. These tools often block network requests to known third-party
  domains, including analytics and engagement platforms. When these requests are
  blocked, you lose critical data visibility, and your users may not receive in-app
  messages or push notifications.
</p>
<p>
  To ensure reliable campaign delivery and campaign performance, MoEngage offers
  the <strong>Custom Proxy Domain</strong> feature. This allows you to route MoEngage
  SDK traffic through a subdomain of your own primary domain (e.g.,
  <code>data.yourcompany.com</code>). Because the traffic appears as first-party
  communication, it bypasses common ad-blocking lists.
</p>
<h2 id="onboarding-process">Onboarding Process</h2>
<p>
  Setting up a Custom Proxy Domain requires a one-time DNS delegation process between
  your team and MoEngage.
</p>

<h3 id="step-1">Step 1: Choose a Subdomain</h3>
<p>
  Select a subdomain that is short and does not contain keywords typically flagged
  by filters (e.g., avoid "tracking", "ads", or "moengage").
</p>
<h3 id="step-2">Step 2: Request DNS Delegation</h3>
<p>
  Reach out to your MoEngage Customer Success Manager (CSM) or
  <a href="https://help.moengage.com" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">raise a support ticket</a>
  to initiate the request. Provide your chosen subdomain.
</p>
<h3 id="step-3">Step 3: Configure NS Records</h3>
<p>
  MoEngage will provide you with a list of Name Server (NS) records. You must add
  these records to your DNS provider's configuration for the chosen subdomain.
</p>
<h2 id="implementation">Implementation</h2>
<p>
  Once the DNS delegation is verified, update your SDK initialization logic. The
  SDK will dynamically rewrite all MoEngage endpoints (API calls and CDN assets)
  to use your custom proxy domain.
</p>

<ol>
  <li data-list-item-id="ec321ba536cde8353d499301d0c60a206">
    <strong>Domain Selection:</strong> Keep your subdomain string short (5-8
    characters).
  </li>
  <li data-list-item-id="ee6bf9166dece0a75e3f6adad47795815">
    <strong>Network Logs:</strong> Verify that requests start with your custom
    subdomain (e.g., <code>sdk-01.data.example.com</code>).
  </li>
