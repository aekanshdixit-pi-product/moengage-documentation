title: "Brand Book"
description: "Manage your brand's visual identity from one place—upload logos, define colors, configure fonts, and set styles for consistent execution across all campaigns."
---

The MoEngage **Brand Book** is a centralized brand hub that manages, stores, and distributes core brand identity assets across the MoEngage marketing platform. This tool enables brands to establish a single source of truth for their visual identity, facilitating consistent execution across multi-channel campaigns (Email, SMS, Push, Landing Pages, Pop-up messages, and In-App Messages).

When you publish brand elements such as logos, colors, fonts, and buttons—your changes apply instantly across all active workspaces and channels.
<Info>
  Each workspace is limited to one active brand book. Currently, this feature is exclusive to **In-App campaigns** and does not support [Portfolio](/user-guide/settings/account/portfolio/portfolio).
</Info>

## Access the Brand Book

The brand book is managed at the account level. To access it, perform the following steps:

1. On the sidebar menu in MoEngage, point to the **Settings** icon <img src="/images/settings-icon.png" alt="Settings icon" style={{height: '20px', display: 'inline', verticalAlign: 'middle'}} />. The Settings menu expands.
2. Click **Account**, and then click **Brand Book**.

   ![Access the Brand Book](/images/access-teh-brand-book.png)

<Info>
  The **Brand Book** is positioned as a standalone option in the navigation pane, separated from standard configuration settings.
</Info>

## Permissions and Access Control

The system enforces role-based access control for managing brand assets. Review your assigned permissions inside your current workspace to verify your access:

| Workspace Account Role | Access Permission Level | Functional Details |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Admin / Manager** | Read, Create, and Modify | Full access to upload brand assets, change fonts, delete files, and publish your brand settings. |

## Create Your Brand Book

When you configure your brand book, select one of the following creation methods:

- [Import Through Website URL](#method-1-import-through-website-url)
- [Manual Setup from Scratch](#method-2-manual-setup-from-scratch)

### Method 1: Import Through Website URL

The platform fetches your visual identity directly from your brand's live website. To import via website URL, perform the following steps:

1. On the **Brand Book** landing page, click **Create brand book**. The **Create brand book** dialog box appears.
2. In the **Enter your brand website URL** box, type or paste your web address (for example, `https://www.example.com`). Leading and trailing whitespace is removed automatically.
3. Click **Import**.

![Import Through Website URL](/images/Import-through-website-URL-.png)

### Method 2: Manual Setup from Scratch

If your target site is protected by corporate access firewalls, or if you prefer full manual control, define your style parameters from scratch:

1. On the **Brand Book** page, click **Create Brand Book**. The **Create brand book** dialog box appears.
2. Click **Enter manually**.

   ![Enter manually option](/images/creat-brand-book23.png)

The brand book page appears, where you can configure your individual brand panels (logos, colors, fonts, and button styles).

## Publish Your Brand Book

Publishing applies your configured brand assets across all your marketing channels. The flow differs slightly the first time you publish versus when you update an already published brand book.

### Publish a New Brand Book

When you finish your initial configuration, perform the following steps:

1. After you configure your assets (logos, colors, fonts, or button styles), click **Publish** in the lower-right corner of the page.
2. In the **Confirm publishing** dialog box, review the summary of the styles that are applied (for example, 2 Hyperlink styles, 3 Brand colors, 1 Brand logo).
3. Click **Confirm** to publish the brand book and apply the styles across your communications.

   ![Confirm publishing](/images/publish-your-brand1.png)

   - **To exit without publishing**: Click **Cancel** or the close icon (×). Your configuration remains in draft mode until you publish it.

   ![Cancel publishing dialog](/images/publish-your-brand-2.png)

### Republish Your Brand Book

When you edit a brand book that has already been published, perform the following steps:

1. After you update or add brand assets, verify that the orange notification banner appears at the bottom of the workspace.

   <Note>To apply your changes, publish your brand book again.</Note>
2. Verify that the yellow **Unpublished Changes** <img src="/images/unpublish.png" alt="Unpublished Changes badge" style={{height: '24px', display: 'inline', verticalAlign: 'middle'}} /> indicator appears in the top header alongside the **Reset brand book** <img src="/images/image-3.png" alt="Reset brand book icon" style={{height: '24px', display: 'inline', verticalAlign: 'middle'}} /> icon.
3. Click **Publish** in the lower-right corner of the workspace.
4. In the **Confirm publishing** dialog box, review the summary of queued changes.
5. Click **Confirm** to publish your updates and apply them across your communications.

   ![Confirm republishing](/images/republish-your-brand1.png)

6. **To exit without publishing**: Click **Cancel** or the close icon (×). Your configuration remains in draft mode until you publish it.
   <Frame>
     ![Republish cancel dialog](/images/republish-your-brand-2.png)
   </Frame>

Published styles are applied across all your communications. You can modify the brand book later if required.

## FAQs

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I create multiple Brand Books for different product lines inside a single workspace?">
    In the current scope of the feature, only a single master brand book can be configured and used across an entire workspace. By default, this master configuration applies across all teams.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why does the website automatic fetch tool sometimes fail to extract my brand logo or corporate font files?">
    Automatic ingestion relies on background crawling scripts. If your website incorporates strict cross-origin resource sharing (CORS) rules, access restrictions, or web application firewalls, the background scanner is blocked from accessing those assets. Logos hosted behind authentication or fonts loaded from private CDNs are also often skipped. In these cases, use the manual workflow to update your brand book assets.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do published brand book changes affect active campaigns and templates inside the editor?">
    Publishing modifications to your brand book immediately updates the default options available inside the asset color pickers and typography menus for new campaigns. However, existing live experiences and pre-saved campaign templates do not update their styled layouts automatically. Changes to existing editor elements must be managed manually within the visual editor interface.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
