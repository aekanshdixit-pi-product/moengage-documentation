This article showcases examples of best practices for generating Jinja code from a natural language query and thereby simplifying your dynamic content implementation. It's important to understand that the Jinja code generated corresponds directly to the details in your natural language query. To achieve optimal results, ensure your natural language queries are structured, include the exact attribute names, and relevant details regarding the desired dynamic content.

## Example

**❌ A bad Jinja description:**

Titles & descriptions from similar items. Truncate descriptions if descriptions are over 50 characters.

**✅ A good Jinja description:**

Show 6 titles & descriptions from Product Set Similar_Items. Truncate descriptions to a length of over 50 characters.
