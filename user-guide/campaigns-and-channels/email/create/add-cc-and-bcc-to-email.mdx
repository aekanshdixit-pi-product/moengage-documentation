---
title: "Add CC and BCC to Email"
---

CC (Carbon copy) and BCC (Blind carbon copy) are used in emails to copy recipients who are not part of the chosen target segment. When this feature is enabled, the CC/BCC recipients will receive the exact copy of the message that the main recipient receives ('To' field)

## Use Cases

- E-commerce/Travel/Hotel brands send email campaigns to sellers, copying the respective account managers.
- Banks copy the customer relationship manager while communicating with premium customers.

## Add CC/BCC Recipients

<Info>
  Adding CC/BCC recipients to an email multiplies the email credits consumed. For example, if you create an email campaign targeting 100 recipients along with one CC and one BCC recipient, use 300 credits instead of 100 as 100 credits are consumed for the main recipient, 100 for the CC recipient, and 100 for the BCC recipient.
</Info>

In Step 2: Content of email campaign creation, click **Cc** or **Bcc** or both to add Cc/Bcc email recipients to a campaign. You can add static addresses or personalized email addresses, as explained below.

1. **Add static Cc/ Bcc addresses**: Every email will have the same set of Cc/Bcc recipients.

![Screenshot 2025-01-20 at 3.05.56 PM.png](/images/moengage_41f807.png)

2. **Add personalized CC/BCC addresses**: The Cc/Bcc email addresses are fetched from the personalized attribute. To personalize the Cc/Bcc, type '@' and click on the **Click to personalize** button (that appears on typing '@' in the Cc and Bcc fields) to insert the personalized Cc/Bcc email attribute.

![mceclip0.png](/images/moengage_616c08.png)

## Impact of Adding Cc/Bcc on Click and Suppression Tracking

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Stats Tracking | Open and click tracking for Cc/Bcc recipients is not captured in campaign stats. If you create an email campaign targeting 100 recipients along with one Cc recipient, then the campaign stats only track the open and click tracking data from the main recipients of the campaign. |
| Suppression Tracking | <ul><li>Whenever a Cc/Bcc recipient unsubscribes or reports an email as spam, it is ignored and not tracked. This is tracked only when the campaign's main recipient unsubscribes or reports an email as spam.</li><li>Whenever a Cc/Bcc recipient encounters a bounce, it is ignored and not tracked. This is tracked only when the campaign's main recipient encounters a bounce.</li></ul> |

## View the Cc/Bcc Recipients for Campaigns

The list of Cc/Bcc recipients added to the campaign is available in the **Campaign Info** \> **Preview and content** section \> **Basic details** for published campaigns. This information can be viewed for each variation.
