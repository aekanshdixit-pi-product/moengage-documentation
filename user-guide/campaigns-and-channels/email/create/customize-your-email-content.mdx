You can customize your email content by adding sender images, Gmail markups, and using custom fonts. This article describes how you can use these options to customize your content for email campaigns.

# Add Sender Image for the Email

To add the sender image for all your email campaigns, use any one of the following methods:

### **Using BIMI**

BIMI is a verification framework that authenticates the brand and enables the display of the brand logo in the avatar field of emails. Yahoo! Mail and [Google](https://cloud.google.com/blog/products/identity-security/bringing-bimi-to-gmail-in-google-workspace) have adopted the BIMI method. For more information on implementing BIMI, refer to [Why, What, and How BIMI](https://bimigroup.org/all-about-bimi/) and [How BIMI differs from other authentication methods.](https://sendgrid.com/blog/what-is-bimi/)

Ensure the following:

- Ensure that DMARC authentication is based on organizational policy. Set it to either **quarantine** or **reject**.
- Publish the BIMI record to your DNS, which has the logo indicator in SVG format or a Verified Mark Certificate (VMC).
- You can contact the MoEngage Support team.

**Using your email account**

To use your email account for adding a sender image, the following prerequisites need to be met:

- - Ensure that your sending address has a mailbox or email account.
  - Ensure that you can easily add a sender image in the mailbox or email account.

Navigate to Settings and use the option to upload or change your sender image. For more information about adding sender images for Outlook and Google, refer to [Outlook](https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/office/change-your-profile-photo-or-name-in-outlook-com-fb6a40cf-0dc8-4ee8-95ae-fa506586f120) and [Google](https://support.google.com/a/users/answer/9308932?hl=en).

# Using Gmail Markups

Markups help users interact with an email in Gmail without opening it by adding call-to-action buttons or links in the subject line. Gmail uses schema.org, a markup vocabulary, an open standard that can be used to add code (markup) to your email templates. With markups, you can highlight important information and improve conversions (click-through rates). For example, you can use Gmail markup to bring up cards in the inbox that highlight the travel information of the user (such as departure time, date, flight information, and so on). Similarly, you can add order confirmation, shipping/delivery information, and reservation details and highlight them or help users take action by adding actions to the email.

<Tip>
  Your domain should be registered with Google for the markups to work. For more information, refer to [Registering with Google](https://developers.google.com/gmail/markup/registering-with-google).
</Tip>

## Add Markups to Email Template

To add a markup to your email template, do the following:

1. Choose the desired markup from the type that you want to add and navigate to it from [here](https://developers.google.com/gmail/markup/reference). For example, to add a one-click action to the email template, navigate to the [one-click action reference page](https://developers.google.com/gmail/markup/reference/one-click-action).
2. Copy the **Micro Data (not the JSON-LD)** for the desired action and paste it to your Email Template in MoEngage during campaign creation.
