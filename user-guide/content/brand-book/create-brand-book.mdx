When configuring your Brand Book, choose one of the following creation methods:

1. **Automatic website ingestion**: The platform scans your public website and pre-fills assets.
2. **Manual asset configuration**: You define every asset by hand.

## Method 1: Import via website URL

The platform fetches your visual identity directly from your brand's live website. To import via website URL, perform the following steps:

1. In the onboarding view, click **Create brand book**.
   The **Create brand book** dialog box appears.
2. In the **Enter your brand website URL** box, type or paste your web address (for example, `https://www.example.com`). Leading and trailing whitespace is removed automatically.
3. Click **Import**.
   ![Import via website URL](/images/brand-book-import.png)

The background scanner analyzes your website's stylesheets and DOM structure to capture official fonts, primary color hex codes, and logo files.

<Info>
  **Import outcomes**

  - **Successful ingestion**: A green success banner displays: "Import Success! Your brand book is ready. Please review everything and publish to go live."
  - **Partial ingestion**: If anti-scraping firewalls block parts of the page, an orange warning banner displays: "Your partial import is ready. You can now modify the imported assets and add any missing details to publish your brand book."
  - **Failed connection**: If the page returns a 404 or 5xx, or the request fails, a red error displays: "We couldn't reach this website. Please check the URL and try again."
</Info>

<Note>
  Automatic ingestion captures what your website exposes publicly. Logos hosted behind authentication, fonts loaded from private CDNs, and assets in custom-rendered components are often skipped. Use **Method 2** to add anything that was missed.
</Note>

## Method 2: Manual setup from scratch

If your target site is protected by corporate access firewalls, or if you prefer full manual control, define your style parameters from scratch:

1. In the **Create brand book** dialog box, click **Enter manually**.
2. The platform opens a blank configuration screen under the **Brand Assets** tab, where you can add individual assets.
