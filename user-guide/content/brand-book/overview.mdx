# Overview

The MoEngage **Brand book** is a centralized repository for managing brand identity assets. This repository helps you maintain a consistent visual and verbal identity across all marketing channels. When you configure standard brand logos, color palettes, typography styles, button attributes, and hyperlink decorators, the changes are automatically applied and can be used across your workspaces.

This uniform presentation improves brand recognition, decreases content creation time, and eliminates the need to manually enter hex codes or reconfigure text formats for every email, push notification, landing page, and in-app message campaign. After publication, your brand book attributes also integrate with the Merlin AI content engine to generate copy that aligns with your brand voice.

# Access the Brand Book

The brand book feature is managed at the account level. To access the Brand book, perform the following steps:

1. On the sidebar menu in MoEngage, hover over the Settings icon ![Settings icon](/images/brand-book-settings-icon.png). The Settings menu expands.
2. Click **Account**, and then click **Brand book**.

<Info>
  **Information**

  The Brand book option is positioned as a standalone option in the navigation pane, separated from standard configuration settings.
</Info>

# Asset Creation Methods

When configuring your brand book, you can choose between the following creation methods:

1. Automatic website ingestion
2. Manual asset configuration.

## Method 1: Import via Website URL

Here, you can instruct the platform to automatically fetch your core visual identity directly from your brand's live website. To import via website URL, perform the following steps:

1. In the onboarding view, click **Create brand book**.

   The **Create brand book** dialog box appears.
2. In the **Enter your brand website URL** box, type or paste your web address (for example, type https://www.example.com). Leading and trailing whitespace is automatically removed.
3. Click **Import**.

   ![Import via website URL](/images/brand-book-import.png)

The background scanner analyzes your website's stylesheets and DOM structure to capture official fonts, primary color hex codes, and logo files.

<Info>
  **URL Import Scenarios and Validations:**

  - **Successful ingestion**: Displays a green success banner: "Import Success! Your brand book is ready. Please review everything and publish to go live."
  - **Partial ingestion**: Triggered if anti-scraping firewalls are active. Displays an orange warning banner: "Your partial import is ready. You can now modify the imported assets and add any missing details to publish your brand book."
  - **Failed connection**: Triggered by network failures, 404, or 5xx response codes. Displays a red error message: "We couldn't reach this website. Please check the URL and try again.
</Info>

## Method 2: Manual Setup from Scratch

If your target site is protected by corporate access firewalls or if you prefer full manual control, you can define your style parameters from scratch by performing the following steps:

1. In the **Create brand book** dialog box, click **Enter manually**.
2. The platform opens a blank configuration screen under the **Brand Assets** tab, where you can add individual assets.

## Configure Brand Assets

The asset manager is divided into visual modules under the **Brand Assets** tab. You can save and publish your details module by module.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Logos">
    Manage your official logos to enable selection inside campaign visual editors. To upload and manage your brand logos, perform the following steps:

    1. In the **Foundation** panel, click **Logo Style** to expand the section.
    2. Click **+ Add Logo** on the right side of the panel.

       The **Upload Logo** dialog box opens.
    3. To upload a local image file:
       1. Click the **Upload** tab.
       2. Drag and drop your logo file into the target zone, or click upload from computer to browse files. The uploader supports .ico, .png, .gif, and .jpg formats, with a maximum file size of 5 MB.
    4. To import an image from a public web server:
       1. Click the **URL** tab.
       2. In the **Add URL here(only 1 URL at a time)** box, type or paste the direct image link (for example, type https://www.example.com/logo.png.
    5. Click **Add Logo** to stage your asset. Verify that the logo is listed in your inventory table. Extremely long filenames in the **Logo File** column are automatically truncated with an ellipsis (...).
    6. (Optional) Under the **Logo Name** column, click the text box to enter a custom descriptor name containing between 5 and 150 characters.
    7. To copy the absolute URL of the asset, click the **Logo URL** icon ![Logo URL icon](/images/brand-book-logo-url-icon.png).
    8. To delete the logo, click the delete icon ![Delete icon](/images/brand-book-delete-icon.png) in the **Actions** column and confirm your choice in the dialog box.

    (Screenshots)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Brand Colors">
    The color management module structures your digital color palettes to prevent off-brand variations. To configure your brand color palettes, perform the following steps:

    1. In the **Foundation** panel, click **Brand Colors** to expand the section.
    2. Click **+ Add Color** on the right side of the panel. A new color row is added to the table.
    3. In the **Color** column, click the color box to open the color picker, or click the text field to type a six-digit hex code (for example, type #000000).
    4. In the **Color Name** column, click the text box and enter a unique descriptor name containing between 5 and 50 characters (for example, enter color_1).
    5. To remove a color from your palette, click the delete icon ![Delete icon](/images/brand-book-delete-icon.png) in the **Actions** column and confirm your choice in the dialog box.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Fonts">
    Upload custom typography files and map fallbacks to maintain consistency when custom fonts cannot be displayed. To upload and configure your custom fonts, perform the following steps:

    1. In the **Foundation** panel, click **Fonts** to expand the section.
    2. Click **+ Add font** on the right side of the panel. The **Add Font** wizard opens.
    3. In step 1 (**Add font files**):
       1. Drag and drop your font files into the target zone, or click **upload from computer** to select them. The uploader supports `.ttf`, `.otf`, `.woff`, and `.woff2` formats, with a maximum file size of 5 MB. You can upload up to 10 font files.
       2. Click **Next**.
    4. In step 2 (**Set fallbacks**):
       1. Choose your fallback priority system across the **Fallback font 1**, **Fallback font 2**, and **Fallback font 3** dropdown menus. These fallbacks will render in your recipient's inbox if their client does not support your custom font.
       2. Click **Save**.
    5. To copy the direct file path of an uploaded font, select the font row in the table and click **Copy font URL**.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Typography">
    Define specific typography styles across five core tags: **Header 1 (H1)**, **Header 2 (H2)**, **Header 3 (H3)**, **Paragraph**, and **Span**.

    To configure your brand typography styles, perform the following steps:

    1. In the **Building Blocks** panel, click **Typography** to expand the section.
    2. Select the typography element you want to modify (for example, click **Header 1** or **Paragraph**).
    3. Under **Font styles,** configure your visual parameters:
       1. In the **Font** list, select your preferred brand typeface (for example, select **Inter**).
       2. In the **Size** box, type a numerical value (for example, type **14**).
       3. In the **Line Height** box, enter a custom spacing value (for example, enter **1.5**).
       4. In the **Unit** list, select either **px** or **rem**.
       5. In the **Font color** box, type a six-digit hex code (for example, type `#000000`) or click the color block.
    4. Under **Style**, select or clear the checkboxes for **Bold**, **Italics** or **Underline**.
    5. Review the changes in the **Preview** section (displays the standard string: _"The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog"_).
    6. Click **Save style**.

    (Screenshots)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Buttons">
    Define layout structures and interactive attributes for up to three distinct button tiers: **Primary**, **Secondary**, and **Tertiary**. To configure your brand button styles, perform the following steps:

    1. In the **Building Blocks** panel, click **Buttons** to expand the section.
    2. Select the button tier you want to modify (for example, click **Primary**, **Secondary**, or **Tertiary**).
    3. To set standard rendering attributes, select the **Default** tab and configure the following parameters:
       1. In the **Font** list, select your preferred brand typeface (for example, select **Inter**).
       2. In the **Size** box, type a numerical value for your font size (for example, type **14**).
       3. In the **Unit** list, select your preferred unit of measure (select either **px** or **rem**).
       4. In the **Font color** box, type a valid six-digit hex code or select the color block.
       5. In the **Background color** box, type a valid six-digit hex code (for example, type **#007bff**) or select the color block.
       6. Under **Alignment**, click the left-align, center-align, or right-align icons to position your button text.
       7. Under **Style**, select the checkboxes for **Bold**, **Italics** or **Underline** to apply specific font decorators.
       8. Under **Border properties**, enter a numerical value in the **Radius** box (for example, enter **0**), select the **Unit**, and type a valid six-digit hex code in the **Border color** box.
       9. Under **Margin**, enter custom numerical values in the **Left**, **Right**, **Top**, and **Bottom** boxes, and select the unit from the **Unit** list.
       10. Under **Padding**, enter custom numerical values in the **Left**, **Right**, **Top**, and **Bottom** boxes (for example, enter **8** for Top and Bottom to pad the text vertically), and select your preferred unit.
    4. To set interactive styling, select the **Hover** tab:
       1. If you want to duplicate your default settings, select the **Copy settings from default state** check box.
       2. If you want to customize your hover behavior, clear the **Copy settings from default state** check box and enter your custom variables (such as changing the background color or adding a border).
    5. Review your configuration in the **Preview** section at the bottom of the section (displays a live rendering button labeled **Button Preview**).
    6. Click **Save style.**

    (Screenshots)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Hyperlinks">
    Define styling specifications for up to two distinct hyperlink variants to ensure consistent clickable elements in your campaigns.

    To configure your hyperlink styles, perform the following steps:

    1. In the **Building Blocks** panel, click **Hyperlinks** to expand the section.
    2. Click the hyperlink variant you want to modify (for example, click **Hyperlink 1** or **Hyperlink 2**).
    3. Click the **Default** tab and configure your base variables under **Font styles**:
       - In the **Font** list, select your preferred typeface (for example, select **Inter**).
       - In the **Size** box, enter a numerical value for your font size (for example, enter **14**).
       - In the **Line Height** box, enter a numerical spacing value (for example, enter **1.5**).
       - In the **Unit** list, select your preferred unit of measure (select either **px** or **rem**).
       - In the **Font color** box, type a valid six-digit hex code (for example, type **#0000EE**) or click the color block.
       - Under **Style**, select or clear the check boxes for **Bold**, **Italics** or **Underline** as required by your design guidelines.
    4. Select the **Hover** tab:
       1. Click the **Copy settings from default state** check box to automatically sync all settings from the **Default** tab.
       2. Click the **Copy settings from default state** check box if you want to define separate hover indicators (such as setting an underline or changing the hyperlink font color on hover).
    5. Verify the layout in the **Preview** section at the bottom of the section (displays a live rendering link labeled **Preview Link**).
    6. Click **Save style** to commit your changes.

## Communication Guidelines

Select the **Communication** tab next to the brand assets panel to define your brand voice, tone, and editorial personality metrics.

This module acts as an instruction engine for the copy platform. When campaign creators use the **Merlin AI** content engine to write copy for email subject lines, push notifications, or SMS bodies, Merlin reads these stored linguistic parameters automatically. This removes the need to manually enter tone variables during copy generation, allowing Merlin to generate copy that aligns with your brand voice.

## Permissions and Access Control

The system enforces role-based access control policies for managing brand assets. Review your assigned permission matrices inside your current workspace to verify your functionality constraints:

| Workspace Account Role         | Access Permission Level   | Functional Details                                                                                                                                                          |
| ------------------------------ | ------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Admin / Manager**            | Read, Create, and Modify  | Grants full access to initialize asset ingestion, modify typography ranges, upload font packages, clear assets, and publish brand book schemas.                             |
| **All Other Workspace Roles**  | Read-Only (View Only mode)| Allows users to view published parameters and select preset assets inside campaign builders. Interactive editing controls, upload fields, and delete icons are dimmed and unavailable. |

# FAQs

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I create multiple Brand books for different product lines inside a single workspace?">
    In the current scope of the feature, only a single master Brand book can be configured and used across an entire workspace. By default, this master configuration applies across all teams. The ability to create multiple independent Brand books and assign access permissions to specific teams within a single workspace is prioritized for a future feature release.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why does the website automatic fetch tool sometimes fail to extract my brand logo or corporate font files?">
    Automatic ingestion relies on automated crawling scripts running in the background. If your target enterprise website incorporates strict cross-origin resource sharing (CORS) rules, access blockades, or web application firewalls, our background scraper will be blocked from accessing those assets. In these instances, utilize the manual workflow to upload your image variants and typography directly.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do published Brand book changes affect active campaigns and templates inside the editor?">
    Publishing modifications inside your Brand book immediately updates the default options available inside the asset color pickers and typography menus for new campaigns. However, existing active live experiences or pre-saved campaign templates will not modify their styled layouts automatically. Changes to existing editor elements must be managed manually within the visual editor interface.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
