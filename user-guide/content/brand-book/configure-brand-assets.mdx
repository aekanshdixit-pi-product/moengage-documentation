The asset manager is divided into visual modules under the **Brand Assets** tab. You can save and publish your details one module at a time. Partial configurations are valid, and you can return to add details later.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Logo Style">
    Manage your official logos to enable selection inside campaign visual editors. To upload and manage your brand logos, perform the following steps:

    1. In the **Foundation** panel, click **Logo Style** to expand the section.
    2. Click **+ Add Logo** on the right side of the panel.

       The **Upload Logo** dialog box opens.
    3. To upload a local image file:
       1. Click the **Upload** tab.
       2. Drag and drop your logo file into the target zone, or click upload from computer to browse files. The uploader supports `.ico`, `.png`, `.gif`, `.svg`, and `.jpg` formats, with a maximum file size of 5 MB.
    4. To import an image from a public web server:
       1. Click the **URL** tab.
       2. In the **Add URL here (only 1 URL at a time)** box, type or paste the direct image link (for example, `https://www.example.com/logo.png`). The URL must point directly to an image file.
    5. Click **Add Logo** to stage your asset. The logo appears in your inventory table. Long filenames in the **Logo File** column are truncated with an ellipsis (`...`); hover to see the full name.
    6. (Optional) Under the **Logo Name** column, click the text box to enter a custom descriptor name containing between 5 and 150 characters.
    7. To copy the absolute URL of the asset, click the **Logo URL** icon ![Logo URL icon](/images/brand-book-logo-url-icon.png).
    8. To delete the logo, click the delete icon ![Delete icon](/images/brand-book-delete-icon.png) in the **Actions** column and confirm your choice in the dialog box.

    <Info>
      You can add up to 10 logos per Brand Book.
    </Info>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Brand Colors">
    The color management module structures your digital color palettes to prevent off-brand variations. To configure your brand color palettes, perform the following steps:

    1. In the **Foundation** panel, click **Brand Colors** to expand the section.
    2. Click **+ Add Color** on the right side of the panel. A new color row is added to the table.
    3. In the **Color** column, click the color box to open the color picker, or click the text field to type a six-digit hex code (for example, `#000000`).
    4. In the **Color Name** column, click the text box and enter a unique descriptor name containing between 5 and 50 characters (for example, `color_1`). Duplicate hex codes are not allowed within the same palette.
    5. To remove a color from your palette, click the delete icon ![Delete icon](/images/brand-book-delete-icon.png) in the **Actions** column and confirm your choice in the dialog box.

    <Info>
      You can add up to 10 colors per Brand Book.
    </Info>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Fonts">
    Upload custom typography files and map fallbacks to maintain consistency when custom fonts cannot be displayed. To upload and configure your custom fonts, perform the following steps:

    1. In the **Foundation** panel, click **Fonts** to expand the section.
    2. Click **+ Add font** on the right side of the panel. The **Add Font** wizard opens.
    3. In step 1 (**Add font files**):
       1. Drag and drop your font files into the target zone, or click **upload from computer** to select them. The uploader supports `.ttf`, `.otf`, `.woff`, and `.woff2` formats, with a maximum file size of 5 MB per file. You can upload up to 10 font files per Brand Book.
       2. Click **Next**.
    4. In step 2 (**Set fallbacks**):
       1. Choose your fallback priority across the **Fallback font 1**, **Fallback font 2**, and **Fallback font 3** dropdown menus. These fallbacks render in your recipient's inbox if their client does not support your custom font.
       2. Click **Save**.
    5. To copy the direct file path of an uploaded font, select the font row in the table and click **Copy font URL**.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Typography">
    Define specific typography styles across five core tags: **Header 1 (H1)**, **Header 2 (H2)**, **Header 3 (H3)**, **Paragraph**, and **Span**.

    To configure your brand typography styles, perform the following steps:

    1. In the **Building Blocks** panel, click **Typography** to expand the section.
    2. Select the typography element you want to modify (for example, **Header 1** or **Paragraph**).
    3. Under **Font styles**, configure your visual parameters:
       1. In the **Font** list, select your preferred brand typeface (for example, **Inter**).
       2. In the **Size** box, type a numerical value (for example, `14`).
       3. In the **Line Height** box, enter a custom spacing value (for example, `1.5`).
       4. In the **Unit** list, select either **px** or **rem**.
       5. In the **Font color** box, type a six-digit hex code (for example, `#000000`) or click the color block.
    4. Under **Style**, select or clear the checkboxes for **Bold**, **Italics**, or **Underline**.
    5. Review the changes in the **Preview** section (displays the standard string: _"The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog"_).
    6. Click **Save style**.

    <Info>
      **Size and line height ranges**

      - When unit is **px**: accepts integer values between 1 and 48.
      - When unit is **rem**: accepts decimal values between 0 and 3.
    </Info>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Buttons">
    Define layout structures and interactive attributes for three button tiers: **Primary**, **Secondary**, and **Tertiary**. To configure your brand button styles, perform the following steps:

    1. In the **Building Blocks** panel, click **Buttons** to expand the section.
    2. Select the button tier you want to modify (**Primary**, **Secondary**, or **Tertiary**).
    3. To set standard rendering attributes, select the **Default** tab and configure the following parameters:
       1. In the **Font** list, select your preferred brand typeface (for example, **Inter**).
       2. In the **Size** box, type a numerical value for your font size (for example, `14`).
       3. In the **Unit** list, select either **px** or **rem**.
       4. In the **Font color** box, type a valid six-digit hex code or select the color block.
       5. In the **Background color** box, type a valid six-digit hex code (for example, `#007bff`) or select the color block.
       6. Under **Alignment**, click the left-align, center-align, or right-align icons to position your button text.
       7. Under **Style**, select the checkboxes for **Bold**, **Italics**, or **Underline** to apply font decorators.
       8. Under **Border properties**, enter a numerical value in the **Radius** box (for example, `0`), select the **Unit**, and type a valid six-digit hex code in the **Border color** box.
       9. Under **Margin**, enter custom numerical values in the **Left**, **Right**, **Top**, and **Bottom** boxes, and select the unit from the **Unit** list.
       10. Under **Padding**, enter custom numerical values in the **Left**, **Right**, **Top**, and **Bottom** boxes (for example, `8` for Top and Bottom to pad the text vertically), and select your preferred unit.
    4. To set interactive styling, select the **Hover** tab:
       1. To duplicate your default settings, select the **Copy settings from default state** check box.
       2. To customize your hover behavior, clear the **Copy settings from default state** check box and enter your custom variables (such as a different background color or border).
    5. Review your configuration in the **Preview** section at the bottom of the section (displays a live rendering button labeled **Button Preview**).
    6. Click **Save style**.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Hyperlinks">
    Define styling specifications for two distinct hyperlink variants to ensure consistent clickable elements in your campaigns. To configure your hyperlink styles, perform the following steps:

    1. In the **Building Blocks** panel, click **Hyperlinks** to expand the section.
    2. Click the hyperlink variant you want to modify (**Hyperlink 1** or **Hyperlink 2**).
    3. Click the **Default** tab and configure your base variables under **Font styles**:
       - In the **Font** list, select your preferred typeface (for example, **Inter**).
       - In the **Size** box, enter a numerical value for your font size (for example, `14`).
       - In the **Line Height** box, enter a numerical spacing value (for example, `1.5`).
       - In the **Unit** list, select either **px** or **rem**.
       - In the **Font color** box, type a valid six-digit hex code (for example, `#0000EE`) or click the color block.
       - Under **Style**, select or clear the check boxes for **Bold**, **Italics**, or **Underline**.
    4. Select the **Hover** tab:
       1. Select the **Copy settings from default state** check box to sync all settings from the **Default** tab.
       2. Clear the **Copy settings from default state** check box to define separate hover indicators (such as adding an underline or changing the font color on hover).
    5. Verify the layout in the **Preview** section at the bottom of the section (displays a live rendering link labeled **Preview Link**).
    6. Click **Save style** to commit your changes.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Publish your Brand Book

Publishing applies your configured brand assets across all your marketing channels. The flow differs slightly the first time you publish versus when you update an already-published Brand Book.

### Publish a new Brand Book

When you finish your initial configuration, perform the following steps:

1. After you configure your assets (logos, brand colors, fonts, typography, buttons, or hyperlinks), click **Publish** in the lower-right corner of the workspace.

   The **Confirm publishing** dialog box opens with a summary of all assets you configured (for example, "2 Hyperlink styles, 3 Brand colors, 1 Brand logo").

   ![Confirm publishing dialog showing the summary of brand assets to be applied](/images/brand-book-confirm-publishing.png)
2. Review the summary of styles that will be applied.
3. Click **Confirm** to publish the Brand Book and apply the styles across your communications.

   To exit without publishing, click **Cancel** or the close icon. Your configuration remains in draft mode until you publish.

### Republish your Brand Book

When you edit a Brand Book that has already been published, perform the following steps:

1. After you update or add brand assets, observe the orange notification banner at the bottom of the workspace: "To apply your changes, publish your brand book again."
2. Confirm the yellow **Unpublished Changes** indicator that appears in the top header next to the **Last Published** timestamp, alongside the **Reset brand book** <Icon icon="rotate-ccw" iconType="regular" /> control.
3. Click **Publish** in the lower-right corner of the workspace.

   The **Confirm publishing** dialog box opens with a summary of all queued changes.
4. Review the summary of styles that will be applied.
5. Click **Confirm** to publish your updates and apply them across your communications.

   To exit without publishing, click **Cancel** or the close icon. Your changes remain in the **Unpublished Changes** state until you publish.

<Info>
  Published styles are applied across all your communications. You can modify the Brand Book later if required.
</Info>
