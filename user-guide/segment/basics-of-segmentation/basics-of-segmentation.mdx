Segmentation lets you define a group of users who share specific properties or behaviors, so you can target them with relevant campaigns or analyze their traits. Instead of messaging your entire user base, you build segments to reach the right people at the right time.

With segmentation, you can answer questions such as:

- Which users purchased at least three times in the last 30 days?
- Who abandoned their cart without completing checkout?
- Which users in Mumbai have not opened the app in two weeks?

<Info>
  **Prerequisites**

  Segmentation relies on user data being available in MoEngage. Ensure that events and user attributes are flowing into your workspace through at least one of the following:

  - [SDK integration](/developer-guide/introduction)
  - [Data APIs](/api/data/data-overview)
  - [Warehouse imports](/user-guide/data/imports/data-warehouses-imports/bigquery-imports)
</Info>

## Building blocks of a segment

Every segment in MoEngage is built from three components: **user attributes**, **events** (with event attributes), and **filters**. These components work together — you pick the data you care about (attributes and events), then apply filters to narrow down to the exact group you need.

### User attributes

User attributes are properties stored on a user's profile, such as their name, email, city, subscription status, or language. MoEngage keeps the latest value and updates it in near real time.

Common examples:

| Attribute | Example value |
| --- | --- |
| Last known city | Mumbai |
| Email subscription | Unsubscribed |
| Push reachability (iOS) | true |
| Language | English |

Some attributes are generated by MoEngage automatically (such as First Seen, Last Seen, and Platform). Your team can also send custom attributes through SDKs or APIs. For details, refer to [Derived Events and Attributes](/user-guide/data/event-data/derived-events-and-attributes).

<Note>
  User attributes store only the current value — they do not retain historical changes. To segment based on what a user *did*, use events instead.
</Note>

### Events and event attributes

Events represent actions a user takes, such as opening the app, viewing a product, or completing a purchase. Each event can carry **event attributes** — details about that specific action, like the product category, price, or screen name.

Unlike user attributes, events are recorded every time a user performs the action, building a history you can query over any time window.

| Event | Event attributes |
| --- | --- |
| Product Purchased | category, price, payment method |
| Page Viewed | page name, referrer, duration |
| Added to Cart | product ID, quantity, color |
| App Opened | app version, platform, device model |

Like user attributes, some events are auto-generated by MoEngage. For the full list, refer to [Derived Events and Attributes](/user-guide/data/event-data/derived-events-and-attributes).

### Filters

Filters are the conditions you apply to attributes and events to narrow down your audience. For example, a filter lets you specify that the city attribute must equal "London," or that the purchase event must have occurred at least three times in the last 30 days.

Filters use operators like `is`, `contains`, `at least`, `in the last`, and `is between` — each tailored to the data type (string, number, date, boolean, etc.). Understanding these operators is essential for building accurate segments.

For a complete reference of all filter operators by data type, refer to [Filters in Segmentation](/user-guide/segment/advanced-concepts/filters-in-segmentation).

### Combining conditions

When your segment needs more than one condition, you combine them with **AND**, **OR**, and **Exclude** joins:

| Join | What it does | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **AND** | Users must match *all* conditions | City is Mumbai **AND** purchased at least once |
| **OR** | Users must match *any* condition | City is Mumbai **OR** city is Delhi |
| **Exclude** | Remove users who match a condition | Purchased at least once **EXCLUDE** unsubscribed from email |

For complex scenarios — such as `(A AND B) OR (C AND D)` — you can nest conditions up to three levels deep. For details on nesting rules and limits, refer to [Segment Nesting](/user-guide/segment/advanced-concepts/segment-nesting).

## Worked example: from question to campaign

Suppose you are an e-commerce marketer who wants to re-engage users in Mumbai who browsed products recently but haven't purchased. Here is how you would move from question to campaign.

**Goal:** *Target users in Mumbai who viewed a product at least twice in the last 7 days but did not purchase.*

<Steps>
<Step title="Define the user attribute condition">
  On the MoEngage dashboard, go to **Segment** > **Create Segments**. Under **User Property**, select:

  - **Last known city** → `is` → **Mumbai**
</Step>

<Step title="Add the behavior condition">
  Under **User Behavior**, add:

  - **Has Executed** → *Product Viewed* → `at least` **2 times** → `in the last` **7 days**

  Then add an **AND** condition:

  - **Has Not Executed** → *Product Purchased* → `in the last` **7 days**
</Step>

<Step title="Check the count">
  Click **Show Count** to see how many users match. The result shows both total users and reachable users by channel (Push, Email, SMS).

  <Check>
  If the count looks reasonable for your use case, the segment is ready. If the count is zero, verify that your events and attributes are being tracked correctly.
  </Check>
</Step>

<Step title="Create a campaign or save for reuse">
  From the results panel, you can either:

  - Click **Create Campaign** to immediately build a push, email, or SMS campaign targeting this segment.
  - Click **Create Custom Segment** to save it for reuse across multiple campaigns and flows.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Build and test your segment logic in a **TEST** workspace before running it in your production environment. This helps you validate the segment count and filters without affecting live campaigns.
</Tip>

## Types of segments

MoEngage supports several segment types for different use cases:

| Type | Best for | How it works |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Rule-based** | Most segmentation needs | Define conditions using attributes, events, and filters. The segment recalculates dynamically each time it runs. |
| **File / Static** | Targeting a known list of users | Upload a CSV with user IDs or emails. The segment contains exactly those users and doesn't change unless you re-upload. |
| **Custom** | Reusing segment logic | Save any segment definition (rule-based, file, or a combination) as a named custom segment for use across campaigns and flows. |

For a detailed comparison and how each type is processed, refer to [Types of Segments](/user-guide/segment/basics-of-segmentation/types-of-segments).
